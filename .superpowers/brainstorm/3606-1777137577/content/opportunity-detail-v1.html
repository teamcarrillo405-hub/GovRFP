Scope of Work

The Sacramento District, U.S. Army Corps of Engineers (USACE) is soliciting proposals for design-build services for Phase III levee infrastructure improvements along the American River Corridor. The selected contractor will be responsible for the complete design, engineering, and construction of approximately 4.2 miles of levee rehabilitation, including earthwork, slope stabilization, drainage improvements, and riprap revetment in accordance with USACE Engineering Manual EM 1110-2-1913 and applicable federal and state flood control standards.

The scope includes geotechnical investigation and seepage analysis, preparation of 100% design drawings and specifications, environmental monitoring and compliance per NEPA and Section 404/401 permit conditions, and coordination with the California Department of Water Resources (DWR) and local reclamation districts. The contractor shall provide a qualified Professional Engineer licensed in the State of California for all structural and geotechnical design submissions. Construction quality control (CQC) documentation, as-built drawings, and a 3-year O&M manual are required deliverables upon project completion.

The period of performance is estimated at 18 months from Notice to Proceed (NTP). The contractor must demonstrate prior experience on USACE or comparable federal levee or civil infrastructure projects within the last 7 years, with individual project values of $1.5M or greater. Offerors are advised that this procurement is set aside exclusively for Women-Owned Small Businesses (WOSB) under FAR 52.219-29, and award will be made on a best-value tradeoff basis considering technical approach, past performance, and price.
