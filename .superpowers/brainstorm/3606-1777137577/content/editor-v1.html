3. Technical Approach

Avero Infrastructure Group proposes a comprehensive, risk-informed technical methodology for the USACE Levee Repair Phase II project at the Sacramento River Control Reach, encompassing approximately 4.3 linear miles of critical flood-control infrastructure. Our approach integrates geotechnical analysis, real-time monitoring instrumentation, and staged construction sequencing to ensure that levee system integrity is maintained throughout all phases of repair activity, with zero interruption to the existing flood-protection baseline during construction operations.

Our technical team will commence mobilization within 14 calendar days of Notice to Proceed, deploying a dedicated crew of 38 personnel including licensed Professional Engineers registered in California, Certified Levee Inspectors (CLI), and USACE EM 385-1-1 Safety Officers. Pre-construction subsurface investigation will include 12 soil borings to depths not less than 30 feet below existing ground surface, continuous CPT soundings at 500-foot intervals, and installation of automated piezometric monitoring wells to establish baseline seepage conditions prior to initiation of any earthwork.

Repair work will be executed in four discrete construction phases aligned with USACE's phased funding structure and seasonal flood-risk constraints. Phase 1 (Stations 0+00 to 12+50) addresses the highest-priority seepage zones identified in the 2024 Periodic Inspection Report, utilizing impervious core augmentation and riverside berm placement to restore factor-of-safety values to not less than 1.5 under static loading conditions. Phase 2 through Phase 4 will be executed on an accelerated critical-path schedule incorporating night-shift earthwork operations, reducing overall project duration by 22 days compared to a standard single-shift baseline schedule.

Material procurement for structural fill and riprap armor will be sourced from USACE-approved borrow sites and quarries within a 45-mile radius of the project site, ensuring material traceability and compliance with Army Corps Engineering Manual EM 1110-2-1913 grading specifications. All earthwork compaction will be verified by a licensed geotechnical testing laboratory under contract to Avero, with density testing at intervals not to exceed one test per 500 cubic yards of placed material. Non-conforming lifts will be reworked immediately at no additional cost to the government, and all test reports will be uploaded to the project quality management system within 24 hours of completion.

Avero will employ a dedicated Environmental Compliance Monitor throughout construction to ensure full adherence to the project's Section 404/401 Nationwide Permit conditions, SWPPP requirements, and USFWS Biological Opinion conditions related to giant garter snake and Swainson's hawk. Daily turbidity monitoring at designated compliance points and real-time reporting to the USACE Contracting Officer Representative will be conducted from first earthwork through final seeding and vegetation establishment.
