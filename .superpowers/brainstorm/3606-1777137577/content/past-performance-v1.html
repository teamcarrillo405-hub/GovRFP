Scope Summary
This contract required full reconstruction of 1.4 miles of earthen levee along the Mississippi River corridor, including subgrade stabilization, riprap armoring, and installation of a new flood-control closure structure. Work was performed under continuous coordination with USACE quality assurance representatives and required strict adherence to EM 1110-2-1913 design standards. The project involved mobilization of heavy civil equipment, dewatering operations, and environmental mitigation measures to protect adjacent wetlands. All work was completed on schedule and within budget, with zero lost-time incidents over the 18-month performance period.
Key Accomplishments
Delivered all 7 contract milestones on or ahead of schedule, earning a schedule performance index of 1.07 at project closeout.
Achieved zero OSHA recordable incidents over 42,000 total labor hours, sustaining a 5-year company-wide EMR of 0.67.
Implemented value engineering proposal that reduced structural riprap quantities by 8%, saving the government $127,000 with no reduction in scope.
Challenges Overcome
Unexpected subsurface anomalies at Sta. 42+00 required rapid geotechnical re-evaluation and a design modification coordinated with USACE engineers within 10 days to avoid schedule impact. The team proactively pre-positioned additional select fill material to maintain continuous earthwork operations while the modification was reviewed and approved.
